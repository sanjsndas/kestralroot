☁️ 1. sky‑high commitment to privacy

Kestral Root Lab (“we”, “our”, “the Lab”) operates under the belief that your personal data deserves the same clarity as a boundless blue sky. This Terms of Privacy document outlines how we collect, use, and protect information when you visit our digital garden (kestralrootlab.example), purchase seeds, or participate in our cloud‑capture workshops. By accessing our services, you enter a relationship rooted in transparency — much like the visible horizon. We never sell your data to third parties; your trust is our most precious heirloom.

Our privacy practices are cultivated in accordance with the VT Consumer Protection Act, GDPR (for European rooters), and the spirit of the open sky. The lab’s data controller can be reached at privacy@kestralrootlab.example or via the phone number below. This document, together with our Privacy Policy and Cookies Policy, forms the complete terms regarding your information.

🌱 2. information we collect – the seed list

We collect only what is necessary to tend your gardening journey:

  • 🌼 identity & contact – name, email, postal address (to ship ‘Kestral Sky’ tomatoes or blue poppy seeds).
  • 💧 payment details – processed via encrypted partners; we never store credit card numbers.
  • 🧪 garden profile – your hardiness zone, soil preferences, favourite blue flowers (optional). This helps us tailor recommendations.
  • 📘 communications – records of workshops, newsletter subscriptions, and inquiries.
  • ☁️ technical data – IP address, browser type, and pages visited, collected through cookies that remember your love for azure blooms.

We do not intentionally collect data from anyone under 16 without parental consent — gardening is for all ages, but privacy is paramount.

💧 3. how we use your data – cloud irrigation

Your information helps us:

  • process seed orders and deliver sky‑blue bulbs to your door.
  • send you seasonal planting reminders (you can unsubscribe anytime).
  • improve our chroma‑cultivation research via aggregated analytics.
  • respond to your questions about indigo milk cap spawn.
  • comply with legal obligations – e.g., tax records for the lab’s non‑profit arm.

We may occasionally use your email to announce a “blue moon” sale or a new batch of Meconopsis, but only if you’ve given us the green (or blue) light. Legitimate interest is balanced with your rights — we never spam.

🪴 4. sharing & reciprocity – like companion planting

We share data only with trusted service providers who help us run the lab (payment processors like Stripe, shipping partners, and email platforms). These providers are bound by strict data terms. We may also disclose information if required by law, or to protect the safety of our gardeners and the integrity of our cloud beds. In the event of a merger or acquisition (unlikely – we’re a family-rooted organisation), your data would transfer under the same privacy commitments.

Importantly, we do not sell or rent your personal information to anyone. No third‑party marketing lists, no trading in personal seeds.

📀 5. international transfers – sky without borders

If you are visiting from outside the United States, please be aware that your data may be transferred to, stored, and processed in the United States (where our servers and blue‑tinted greenhouses are located). Data protection laws in the U.S. may differ from those in your country. By using our services, you consent to this transfer. For European users, we implement Standard Contractual Clauses and maintain adequate safeguards.

🔒 6. data security – our root cellar

We protect your data with industry‑standard encryption (SSL), firewalls, and strict access controls. Our staff sign confidentiality agreements and are trained in privacy gardening — they water only what’s needed. In the unlikely event of a breach, we will notify affected individuals within 72 hours as required by law and provide guidance (like covering tender seedlings).

⏳ 7. retention – perennials, not annuals

We retain your personal information only as long as necessary: order history is kept for tax purposes (usually 7 years); newsletter subscriptions until you unsubscribe; garden profiles may be anonymised after 3 years of inactivity. You can request deletion at any time (see section 10).

🍪 8. cookies & similar technologies – tiny blue chips

Our website uses cookies to remember your favourite blue varieties and analyse traffic. Essential cookies keep you logged in; preference cookies remember if you like delphiniums or salvias. You can manage cookie settings via your browser. For full details, see our separate Cookies Policy.

📩 9. marketing choices – opt out like a cloud

You may opt out of promotional emails at any time by clicking “unsubscribe” or emailing us. Even if you unsubscribe, we may still send non‑promotional messages (order confirmations, workshop receipts).

⚖️ 10. your rights – dig deep

Depending on your location, you may have the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of your personal data. To exercise these rights, please contact us using the details in the footer. We’ll respond within 30 days (and often sooner, like a fast‑growing radish). For VT residents, we comply with the Vermont Consumer Protection Act; for California, the CCPA; for Europe, GDPR. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

🌻 11. children’s privacy

Our website and workshops are intended for general audiences, but we do not knowingly collect data from children under 13 (or 16 in Europe). If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so we can delete it.

📅 12. changes to these terms – seasons change

We may update this Terms of Privacy occasionally — for instance, to reflect new gardening practices or legal requirements. The latest version will always be posted here, with the “last revised” date at the top. If changes are significant, we’ll notify you by email or a banner on our site. Your continued use after such changes constitutes acceptance.

☔ 13. specific provisions for workshop participants

If you register for a “sky‑blue rain garden” or “indigo dye” workshop, we collect emergency contact info and any dietary restrictions (for our blueberry snacks). This data is used solely for workshop logistics and deleted within 60 days after the event. Photos taken during workshops may be used for promotional materials only with your explicit consent.

🧪 14. research data and anonymised information

Kestral Root Lab conducts agricultural research. We may aggregate and anonymise data (e.g., “72% of participants saw increased pollinator visits after planting borage”). Such anonymised data is not considered personal information and may be used for publications or shared with partner universities.
